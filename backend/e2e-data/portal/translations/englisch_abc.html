Dummy Data Regulation

(Abc)

Full citation: Dummy Data Regulation published on 13 October 2025 (Federal Law Gazette I p. 99) as last amended by Article 99 of the Act of 23 October 2025 (Federal Law Gazette 2025 I No. 999)

Section 1
Dummy data generation, requirement for a permit

(1) Entities generating dummy data for commercial purposes must have a permit. Data is considered dummy if it is not linked to any real-world person or entity. The generation of such data is only permissible if a verifiable process is in place to ensure no accidental inclusion of real data. The commercial use of dummy data is permissible for a maximum duration of 18 consecutive months. Before a project starts, the entities must designate the data as dummy and document the process for its creation.

(2) If data is generated for third parties and the generating party does not assume the customary obligations of data security, it is assumed that the generating party is providing a data-as-a-service.

(3) This Regulation does not apply to dummy data generated for personal use or for educational purposes.

Section 2
Granting and expiry of permits

(1) The permit is granted upon a written application.

(2) The permit is valid for one year. An application for renewal must be submitted no later than one month before the expiration date. The permit may be renewed for another year unless the approval authority denies the renewal. The permit expires if not used for three years.

Section 3
Denial

(1) The permit or its renewal will be withheld if there is a justified assumption that the applicant fails to meet the required security standards or has previously violated data protection laws.

(2) The permit may be withheld if the applicant is not a German national or if a company is not registered under German law.

Section 4
Regulatory offences

(1) Regulatory offences are committed by parties that wilfully or negligently:

1. Generate dummy data without a permit in contravention of section 1.

2. Use real data instead of dummy data when a permit is required.

3. Fail to document the data generation process correctly.

(2) Regulatory offences are punishable by a fine of up to 50,000 euros.
