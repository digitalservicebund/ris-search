Act on Chemistry and Dentists

(Zahnarzt-Chemie Gesetz – CDe)

Translation provided by Max Musterman for the Federal Ministry for Everything

Version information: Act on Chemistry and Dentists as published on 15 July 1985 (Federal Law Gazette I, p. 2010)

Section 1
Scope of application

(1) This Act applies to the handling of all chemical substances used in dental practices for diagnostic, therapeutic, or prosthetic purposes. It also governs the professional duties of dentists in relation to these substances.

(2) The Act does not apply to household cleaning products or food items found in a dental practice.

Section 2
Permits and training

(1) Any dentist using regulated chemical substances must hold a valid permit. The permit is issued by the Federal Institute for Dental and Chemical Safety upon successful completion of a certified training course.

(2) The training must be renewed every five years to ensure up-to-date knowledge of chemical safety protocols and new substance regulations.

Section 3
Storage and disposal

(1) Regulated chemical substances must be stored in a secure, clearly marked cabinet, away from foodstuffs and patient areas. A log of all substances and their usage must be maintained.

(2) The disposal of chemical waste must follow strict guidelines to prevent environmental contamination. Failure to comply with disposal regulations is a serious offence.

Section 4
Regulatory offences

(1) Regulatory offences are committed by parties that wilfully or negligently:

1. Use regulated chemical substances without a permit.

2. Fail to store or dispose of substances in accordance with this Act.

3. Fail to maintain an accurate usage log.

(2) Regulatory offences are punishable by a fine of up to 10,000 euros for each instance.
